Celso Vieira, is an assistant researcher under the supervision of Prof. Barbara Sattler at the Ruhr Universität Bochum. He mostly works on the Presocratics and Plato. His previous work focused on the role of style in Heraclitus, as shown in this paper published in the Classical Quarterly . Currently, he studies the tools and concepts of process metaphysics to develop an interpretation of Heraclitus as a process philosopher without unnecessary committments to a substance framework. AEON published his introductory essay about process metaphysics. It was later translated into Russian.

Before coming to Bochum, Celso received a grant by the Andrew Mellon Foundation to research the Vlastos Archive in the Harry Ransom Center at the University of Texas at Austin. He worked with Prof. Alexander Mourelatos examining Vlastos’ notes, drafts papers and correspondence to reconstruct his method for the history of philosophy.

A testimonial of the visit can be found at the Harry Ransom Magazine. The paper was published in De Gruyter's Elenchos.

Celso has also worked extensively with Plato. As a post-doctoral researcher at the UFPA (Brazil), he has investigated the pedagogical role of lying in Plato. The outcome of the investigation can be found in Brill's Méthexis.

He has translated Plato’s Cratylus for the Brazilian Publisher Paulus. The translation was briefly reviewed at the HuffPost (Greek). His translation of the Alcibiades I is planned to be out in 2022 by Penguim/ Cia das Letras.

During the ungraduate studies at UFMG (Brazil), the lectures from Prof. Jacyntho Brandão inspired Celso to pursuit his studies in ancient Greek. The contact with Prof. Miriam Peixoto in the philosophy department sparked his interest on Presocratic Studies. She supervised his graduate studies. Celso also spent a semester as a visiting student at the University of Toronto under the supervision of Prof. Rachel Barney funded by CAPES.
